Business Skill | Verbal Communication

Mastering Professional Phone Etiquette

A collection of **essential phrases** for initiating, managing, and concluding confident business phone conversations.

Series 1: Initiation and Transfer (Getting to the Right Person)

Requesting a Speaker and Stating Purpose

  • May I speak to [Person] please? The simple, polite way to ask a receptionist/assistant for a specific person.
  • Can you put me through to [Person/Department] please? Asking for the call to be transferred to a specific colleague or department (e.g., Customer Service).
  • I'm calling regarding/about [Subject]. Clearly stating the reason for your call at the beginning (e.g., "...a late shipment"). *'Regarding' is slightly more formal.*

Handling Wrong Numbers and Names

  • Sorry, I think I have the wrong number. A polite alternative to panicking and hanging up when you've misdialed.
  • I'm afraid I didn't catch your name. / Sorry, I didn't catch your name. A polite way to ask someone to repeat their name, often used at the end of the call for reference.

Tip:

Using "I'm afraid I..." softens the delivery, making it sound more apologetic and professional when asking for personal information.

Series 2: Availability and Messages (When the Timing is Off)

Managing Your Own Interruptions

  • Sorry, it's not a good time. I'm driving/walking into a meeting/about to jump on a call. Can you call me back in one hour? A complete phrase for turning down a call immediately while providing the reason and a clear alternative time.
  • Could you please call me back at [Phone Number]? The direct way to request a return call. The 'at' is short for 'you can reach me at'.

Offering and Taking Messages

  • Can I leave a message for [Name] please? When the person you want to speak to is unavailable, you can ask their assistant to relay information.
  • I'm sorry, [Name] is not available at the moment/right now. Can I take a message? / Can I pass a message along? The standard, polite way to inform a caller that someone is out of office or in a meeting, and offer assistance.

Series 3: Clarity and Holding (Maintaining Control)

Ensuring Audio Quality and Clarity

  • Can you hear me okay? / Can you hear me clearly? Checking that the background noise or poor connection isn't hindering communication. *'Clearly' is more formal than 'okay'.*
  • Could you repeat that please? Use this to politely ask the speaker to say something again—whether you misheard a number, didn't understand an acronym, or need them to spell a word.

Requesting the Speaker to Wait

  • Would you mind holding for a moment while I double check that for you? The professional way to ask someone to wait, clearly stating the reason. *Avoid abrupt phrases like "Hold on" or "Wait a minute."*

Series 4: Seeking and Follow-up (Next Steps)

Requesting Information

  • I would like some information about [Subject]. A slightly formal way to ask for more details on a product or service (e.g., a "brunch special" or "new offering").
  • Can you tell me about [Subject] please? A more conversational alternative to the formal request above.

Maintaining Contact on Ongoing Issues

  • I am calling to follow up on [Issue]. The standard, professional way to re-engage on a delayed or unresolved issue, such as a missing shipment or a refund.

Consolidation Exercise: Phone Call Scenarios

Compose short dialogue snippets for the following scenarios, using at least one phrase from each of the sections above.

Scenario 1: Escalation and Hand-off

You call a supplier regarding a late shipment, but the person you need is unavailable. Ask to be transferred to the correct department and leave a message.

Scenario 2: Clarity and Follow-up

You are calling back to check on a delayed refund, but the connection is poor and you need the representative to wait while you pull up the order number.

Scenario 3: Managing a Call while Driving

Someone calls you with urgent questions, but you are driving. You briefly check if they can hear you, decline the conversation, and ask them to call you back later.

FINAL WORD | Confidence in Delivery

Professional phone etiquette is about maintaining **control** and **clarity**. By using precise, polite language for transfers, interruptions, and information requests, you sound confident and respectful in every conversation.
